                                                                                                                         Abstract: Compensatory selection is generally more reliable than multiple-hurdle selection. Yet, practitioners may lean toward multiple-
                                                                                                                         hurdle models, because administering an entire predictor battery to every applicant can be time-consuming, labor-intensive, and costly. Using
                                                                                                                         Monte Carlo simulation, we considered some specific cases to illustrate, in terms of selection utility and the cost-reliability tradeoff between
                                                                                                                         compensatory and multiple-hurdle selection models. Results showed that compensatory model selection produced a higher level of expected
                                                                                                                         criterion performance in the selected applicant subgroup, and a higher overall selection utility in most conditions. The simulation provides
                                                                                                                         researchers and practitioners with a practical illustration of the tradeoff between reliable (compensatory) versus cost-efficient (multiple-
                                                                                                                         hurdle) selection models – one that can inspire the exploration of other scenarios and tradeoffs.

                                                                                                                         Keywords: selection utility, selection model, high-stakes testing, Monte Carlo simulation
